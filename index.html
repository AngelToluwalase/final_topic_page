Studio Ghibli

Spirted Away (2001)

This Oscar-winning film follows a young girl, Chihiro, who stumbles into a magical world and must work at a bathhouse for spirits to save her parents. It’s a tale of transformation, both literally and emotionally, filled with rich symbolism, strange creatures, and commentary on growing up, consumerism, and identity. Fun fact: the character No-Face represents how people absorb the emotions and behaviors of those around them!

Kiki's Delivery Service (1989)

A heartwarming coming-of-age story about a young witch named Kiki who moves to a seaside town to begin her training. As she starts a delivery service on her flying broomstick, Kiki faces self-doubt and burnout—relatable themes even today. This film beautifully shows how creative energy ebbs and flows. Plus, Jiji the cat is a fan favorite!

Howles moving castle (2004)

This whimsical story centers on Sophie, a hat maker turned old woman by a witch’s curse, and the mysterious wizard Howl. Set against a war-torn backdrop, the movie explores beauty, transformation, and anti-war sentiments. The castle itself is a magical steampunk mash-up that reflects Howl’s personality—messy, powerful, and constantly in motion.
